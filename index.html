Dr. Aklilu Lemma

The nightmare of Bilharzia

Aklilu Lemma (1935-1997) was a young Ethiopian doctor who discovered a natural treatment against bilharzia, also known as schistosomiasis, a debilitating and eventually fatal illness. He relentlessly studied and systematically improved his research, developing an affordable preventative, locally controllable means of eradicating a disease that, after malaria, has been the second greatest scourge in the Third World.

The Time Line of Dr. Aklilu Lemma

1934 - Born in Ethiopia
1955 - Graduates from Addis Ababa University with a degree in Biology
1964 - Earns a Ph.D. in Parasitology from Johns Hopkins University
1964 - Discovers that the Endod plant (African soapberry) can be used to control schistosomiasis, a deadly parasitic disease affecting millions in Africa
1970s - Conducts extensive research on Endod, proving its effectiveness as a natural molluscicide, which kills the snails that spread schistosomiasis
1989 - Receives the Right Livelihood Award (Alternative Nobel Prize) for his pioneering work in tropical medicine and disease control
1990s - Works to promote local, affordable, and sustainable disease prevention methods in Africa
1997 - Passes away, leaving behind a legacy in global health and parasitology

"We have learned the hard way that the root problems of scientific research in Africa are not only the lack of facilities and funds but also the biases of individuals and organisations in industrialised countries." --Aklilu Lemma, 1989 Laureat"
